# Clean993 Product Marketing Context

## Product

Clean993 is a personal technical blog and X account for Chinese-speaking software engineers. The blog publishes engineering notes, technical sharing, and implementation retrospectives at `https://clean99.github.io`; X is the distribution and feedback surface.

This is not a generic creator account. The promise is practical engineering judgment: one reusable rule, mechanism, or failure mode that a reader can apply in their own work.

## Current Objective

Grow `@Clean993` by 1000 followers in one week from the baseline recorded in `data/social-growth/ledger.json`.

Primary conversion path:

1. Image-backed X post makes the mechanism obvious in the feed.
2. X Article or thread delivers the complete technical frame inside X.
3. Blog link appears only after the X-native value has been delivered.
4. Profile promise and pinned post convert profile visits into follows.

## Audience

Primary readers are Chinese-speaking frontend engineers, full-stack engineers, engineering leads, and AI-assisted coding practitioners.

They care about:

- practical software engineering quality;
- performance measurement and optimization loops;
- testing, validation, and release discipline;
- AI agent workflows that produce verifiable engineering output;
- technical writing that leaves a reusable frame instead of a vague opinion.

## Positioning

Clean993 turns real engineering work into reusable technical judgment.

Good content should answer at least one of these:

- What false signal misleads engineers?
- What mechanism makes the result repeatable?
- What evidence would prove the claim?
- What tradeoff should a senior engineer notice?
- What checklist can the reader use tomorrow?

## Voice

- Direct, specific, technical, slightly sharp.
- Prefer observed failure and mechanism over generic advice.
- Use short X lines and one idea per line.
- Make claims concrete enough that a senior engineer can disagree.
- Avoid corporate slogans, balanced AI templates, and empty encouragement.

## Calls To Action

Preferred CTAs:

- follow for engineering notes that turn real work into reusable rules;
- open the X Article/thread for the complete frame;
- read the blog link after the X-native explanation.

Avoid:

- raw blog links in the first short post;
- "I wrote an article" as the hook;
- engagement bait such as asking for likes, reposts, or generic opinions;
- mass replies, mass likes, follow/unfollow tactics, or unrelated trend hijacking.
