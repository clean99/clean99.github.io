## Audience

Write for Chinese-speaking software engineers, frontend engineers, engineering leads, and AI-assisted coding practitioners who care about practical engineering quality.

The ideal reader follows when a post gives them one reusable judgment rule, one implementation frame, or one concrete mistake to avoid in their own work.

## Voice

- Direct, specific, technical, and slightly sharp.
- Prefer observation from practice over generic advice.
- Use short lines on X. One idea per line.
- Make claims concrete enough that a senior engineer can disagree with them.
- Keep the first post readable without the blog link.

## Content Pillars

- AI-assisted software engineering workflows
- Performance optimization and measurement loops
- Frontend engineering quality
- Testing, validation, and release discipline
- Technical writing that leaves reusable frames
- Blog-to-X repurposing with image-first packaging

## Platform Rules

- X short posts should sell the insight first. Do not lead with the blog URL.
- Prefer image-backed first posts, then thread replies or an X Article for depth.
- Blog links go at the end of the X Article or late in the thread, never as the opening payload.
- Metrics that matter: followers, profile visits, replies, reposts, bookmarks, likes, impressions, link clicks.
- Public actions still require the `x-growth-publishing` browser confirmation boundary.

## Avoid

- "不是 X，而是 Y" template hooks.
- "我写了一篇..." openings unless the sentence itself contains a strong claim.
- Inflated words: 本质上, 深度, 完整闭环, 赋能, 体系化, 全面改造, 颠覆.
- AI handoff phrases: 总结一下, 接下来我们, 值得注意的是, 这意味着.
- Generic claims that could fit any technical blog post.
- Hashtag stuffing. Use 0-2 hashtags only when they clarify distribution.
